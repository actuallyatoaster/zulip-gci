#Zulip Portuguese Research
Most commonly, the language used is slightly more formal than
conversational, especially conjugating the imperative with "você" instead
 of the more conversational "tu".

Many verbs, especially menu options, are left unconjugated ("Configurar",
"Sair" etc.) but this is rather inconsistent and the imperative tense is
also used often. Reflexive verbs are almost never used.

Many websites offer options for both Brazilian and European portuguese,
but those that don't usually use formal Brazilian portuguese.

Words that are part of the service (like Twitter's "Moments")
are just used in English. This would apply to many of Zulips words such
as "streams".

I've observed that Portuguese tends to take up slightly more space to say
the same thing as English does. This may affect the interface.

Portuguese contains multiple special/non-english characters : ã/Ã á/Á à/À
â/Â ç/Ç é/É ê/Ê í/Í õ/Õ ó/Ó ô/Ô ú/Ú
